User Stories

1. Integrate with other tools

1.1 Calendar & Scheduling Access

As an accountant with a full time job and children, I would like the ability to schedule my runs so that I can get in
the habit of running daily.

Elaboration: Users should be able to schedule their tasks throughout their daily lives.
We will have a calendar that they can sync with their life and work calendars to make scheduling easier.

Constraints: A constraint would be whether the user's current calendar app can integrate data with our app without
issues of legality or liability.

Effort Estimation: 4 person-hours.

Acceptance Test: Perform syncing of Google Calendar & phone's personal calendar to the app.
After syncing is complete, schedule a task and ensure that scheduled task shows up on all synced calendars.

1.2 Long term Calender

As a student, I would like to learn how to time management with the habit tracker app.

Elaboration: I have been having issues with keeping track of time for my assignments and study times for my university.
I would like to be able to use this app to be able to remind and motivate me to complete my assignments.
This will require a calendar to be used to write down the daily reminders.

Constraints: Grades should be passing, which is a c or above.

Effort Estimation: 1 Person-Hours

Acceptance Test: Insert into calendar to remind me daily to finsh all of my assignments for the day.
Check grades and achievments page to see how progress is being made.

2. Positive Reinforcement

2.1 Rewards as Motivation

I am a reward motivated young adult and I would like to get in the habit of doing the dishes before I go to bed.

Elaboration:Users will receive achievement tokens each time they reach a milestone in forming their habit.
This positive reinforcement tool will aid those who are more reward motivated to continue performing tasks each day in
the hopes of a *new* reward.

Constraints: The constraints will be the storage capability of our app. We need to have enough reward tokens to be able
to send back to the user but not so many that our performance is slowed by too much data stored.

Effort Estimation: 15 Person-Hours

Acceptance Test: With each milestone a reward token is outputted to the user. With each habit fully formed, a larger
more delectable reward is outputted.

2.2 Recognition

As a student athlete who appreciates recognition for achievements, I would like to get in the habit of drinking
more water throughout the day.

Elaboration:As users reach milestones along their journey to creating a new habit they will receive some kind of
medal/trophy in recognition of their achievements. Positive reinforcement has been shown to keep people motivated to
continue a task.

Constraints: The constraints will be the storage capacity of our app, and accurately keeping track of user milestone
achievements.

Effort Estimation: 10 Person-Hours

Acceptance Test: At each milestone reached, a reward trophy will be given to the user.
When a habit is considered completed the user will receive the highest reward trophy for that habit.

3. Daily Progress Report

3.1 Graphical Data

As a graphic designer, I love seeing data represented in interesting ways but have trouble finding the time to eat
breakfast.

Elaboration: Users should be able to track their progress in more than one way and a graphical display can give them
better understanding of their daily progress.

Constraints: Our database needs to be able to hold the habits and daily actions in order to be able to output a correct
graph.
We need to be able to properly query the data and output the graph.

Effort Estimation: 10 Person-Hours

Acceptance Test: An accurate graph is outputted as the user inputs information.

3.2 Graphs

As a data analyst, I enjoy looking at hard data and graphs that tell me a story.

Elaboration:A user should be able to track how well they’re doing on any individual habit, and see that data in an
easy to digest manner such as graphs.

Constraints: Our database needs to be able to hold all of the information for a user and their habits, and be easily
accessible to show relevant data while also being secure enough that people’s information cannot leak.

Effort Estimation: 15 Person Hours

Acceptance Test: A user can see a graph of how well they are completing their individual habits.

4. Notification Systems

4.1. Reminders

As a busy student athlete, I would appreciate getting a quick reminder of an activity earlier enough so I do not
forget or so that I am not late -
i.e. a reminder to drink water every half hour can be important on busy schedule times.

Elaboration:Users should be able to receive a quick reminder or a notification of the activities that they are
supposed to get done on time, say at a set interval of time or a specific time during the day.
Constraints: our app should make it easy to turn off reminders and notifications if a user does not need it anymore,
or add on to reminders according to their needs

Effort Estimation: 20 person hours
Acceptance Test: The user-set reminders accurately pop up on screen for specific habits.

4.1 Time management/ organization

As a college student who struggles with time management and staying on track, a habit tracking app would be helpful
in organizing my day more efficiently.

Elaboration:with our app's ability to track study habits and set reminders for important deadlines, users will be able
to stay on track with her study, assignments and exams. The streak feature showing progress helps to keep her motivated
and on task.

Constraints: Our app needs to be able to keep track of each activity and manage the reminders and the streaks

Effort Estimation: 5 Person Hours
Acceptance Test: Streak shows proper progress and activity for each habit set.
